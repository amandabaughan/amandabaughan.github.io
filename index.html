Amanda Baughan

Hello! I am a third year PhD student in Computer Science and Engineering at the University of Washington. I'm a member of Alexis Hiniker's User Empowerment Lab. I study how people argue online, and I'm interested in empowering users to have difficult conversations in online spaces. My work draws from a variety of techniques in HCI, data science, and NLP, and is informed by work in ethics, sociology, and psychotherapy.

Previously, I worked as a UX Researcher at AI2, studying how users navigate Semantic Scholar and their recommender models. Before that, I spent two years working at Google where I spent my 20% time working on UX Research projects on Duo and Google Cloud.

When not working, you can find me in a yoga studio, attending startup talks, or exploring the PNW.
